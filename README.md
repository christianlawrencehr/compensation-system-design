# Compensation System Design (E-sonic Case Study)

This project demonstrates the design of a comprehensive compensation architecture for E-sonic, a digital music distribution company. The system integrates job evaluation, market pricing, pay structure design, and performance-based compensation policies.

## Project Objectives

• Establish internal pay equity through a structured job evaluation system  
• Align compensation with external labor market data  
• Design transparent salary ranges and pay grades  
• Implement a merit pay system that rewards performance while maintaining budget discipline  

## Key Components

### 1. Point-Factor Job Evaluation

A structured job evaluation model was developed using four compensable factors:

• Accountability (40%)  
• Know-How (30%)  
• Problem Solving (20%)  
• Innovation (10%)

Each job is evaluated on a five-degree scale and assigned a total score out of 1000 points, enabling objective comparisons across roles.

### 2. Market Pricing and Regression Analysis

Regression analysis was used to estimate market salaries based on job evaluation points. The model calculates the expected salary for each role while accounting for variation across job families.

This approach ensures compensation decisions are data-driven and aligned with external labor markets.

### 3. Pay Grades and Salary Ranges

Jobs were grouped into pay grades based on evaluation scores. Salary ranges were created using market policy lines to determine competitive compensation levels.

### 4. Merit Pay System

A merit pay system was designed with a target budget of 3% of total payroll. Employees receive performance-based increases based on salary quartile and performance ratings.

The final projected merit pay increase totaled **2.956% of payroll**, remaining within the established budget while rewarding high performers.

### 5. Contribution-Based Pay Policies

Different compensation philosophies were applied based on job family:

• Administrative: Seniority-based pay  
• Sales & Marketing: Incentive-based pay  
• Customer Service: Merit-based pay  
• Software Development: Person-focused pay  

This structure aligns compensation strategy with the unique characteristics of each job function.
